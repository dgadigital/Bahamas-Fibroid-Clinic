Understanding Fibroids

We’ve got all the informationyou need to know about Fibroids

What are fibroids?
  • Nature: Benign (non-cancerous) tumors made of muscle and fibrous tissue.
  • Location: Can grow inside the uterus, on its outer surface, or within its wall.
  • Size: Vary from microscopic to large masses that can enlarge the uterus.
Do fibroids go away on their own?

Fibroids may shrink after menopause due to a decrease in hormone production but don’t necessarily disappear completely.

What are the common symptoms of fibroids?
  • Heavy menstrual bleeding
  • Menstrual periods lasting more than a week
  • Bloating, pelvic pressure or pain
  • Frequent urination, urinary urgency, or difficulty emptying the bladder
  • Constipation
  • Backache or leg pains
  • Pain associated with intercourse
  • Miscarriages
What are the causes of fibroids?

The exact cause of fibroids is unknown, but factors may include genetic changes, hormonal imbalances (estrogen and progesterone promote their growth), and other growth factors.

What are the risk factors of fibroids?
  • Age (more common in women in their 30s and 40s)
  • Family history of fibroids
  • Ethnic origin (more common in women of African-American and Asian descent)
  • Obesity
  • Dietary factors (high red meat consumption, low green vegetable intake)
How are fibroids diagnosed?

Fibroids are typically diagnosed through a pelvic examination, ultrasound, or MRI.

What are the treatment options for fibroids?
  • Medications: To manage symptoms, such as hormonal treatments that regulate menstrual cycles or target hormones that regulate fibroid growth.
  • Non-invasive procedures: MRI-guided focused ultrasound surgery (FUS), using sound waves to destroy fibroid tissue.
  • Minimally invasive procedures: Including uterine artery embolization (UAE), which kills fibroids by cutting off their blood supply, and myolysis, which uses electric current or laser to destroy fibroids and shrink blood vessels.
  • Surgical options: Myomectomy (removal of fibroids, preserving the uterus) or hysterectomy (removal of the uterus, the definitive solution but prevents future pregnancies).

I am so glad I am a woman of great faith!!! In spite  of my doctor’s advice and without a referral letter, with the support of my husband, I decided to make an appointment to see Dr. Higgins.
